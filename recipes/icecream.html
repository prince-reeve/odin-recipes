Icecream - Homemade

Ice cream is a frozen dessert made from cream, milk, and sugar, often with added flavors and mix-ins such as fruit, nuts, chocolate, and candy. It is typically churned to create a creamy and smooth texture, and can be served in a cone, cup, or dish. Ice cream has a long history, with variations of the treat dating back to ancient China, Greece, and Rome. Today, ice cream is a popular dessert around the world and can be found in many different forms, from soft serve to hand-crafted artisanal varieties. It can also be used in various desserts like sundaes and milkshakes.

recipe - Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • Pinch of salt

instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cream, milk, sugar, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is hot.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks together. Slowly pour some of the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks while whisking constantly to temper the eggs.
  3. Pour the egg yolk mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream mixture and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any cooked egg bits.
  6. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, making sure the plastic wrap is touching the surface of the mixture to prevent a skin from forming.
  7. Refrigerate the mixture for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  8. Once chilled, churn the mixture in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  9. Transfer the churned ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.
  10. Scoop and serve the homemade vanilla ice cream as desired.
