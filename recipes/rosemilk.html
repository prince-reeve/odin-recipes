Rose milkshakes

Rose milk, also known as "gulab jamun milk" or "gulab jamun ki kulfi", is a traditional Indian sweet beverage made by mixing rose syrup and milk. The syrup is made by boiling sugar, water and rose petals to create a thick sweet syrup. The syrup is then mixed with milk to create the drink. The drink is often flavored with cardamom powder and saffron. Some variations also include the addition of nuts like pistachios and almonds. The drink is often served chilled, making it a perfect summer refreshment. It is popular in South Asian countries like India, Pakistan and Bangladesh. Rose milk is also a popular flavor of ice cream, kulfi and other desserts.

recipe - Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup rose syrup
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • a few saffron strands (optional)

instructions

  1. In a pan, heat the milk over medium heat until it comes to a simmer.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and add the rose syrup, cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using). Stir well to combine.
  3. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the milk is infused with the rose and cardamom flavors.
  4. Remove the pan from the heat and let the milk cool to room temperature.
  5. Once cooled, strain the milk through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any petals or saffron strands.
  6. Chill the milk in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until cold.
  7. Serve the rose milk chilled, garnished with a sprinkle of cardamom powder or saffron strands (optional).
