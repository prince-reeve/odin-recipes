BIRIYANI - Indian Style

Biryani is a popular dish from the Indian subcontinent, made with seasoned rice, meat (such as chicken, beef, or lamb), and a variety of spices. The dish is often served with raita (a yogurt-based condiment) and chutney. Biryani can also include vegetables and is known for its unique blend of flavors and aromas. It is often served as a main course at special occasions and celebrations.

instructions

To make chicken biryani, you will need 2 cups of basmati rice, 4 cups of chicken broth or water, 1 lb of boneless, skinless chicken thighs, 2 onions, 2 cloves of garlic, 1 tablespoon of ginger, 2 teaspoons each of ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, cardamom, 1/2 teaspoon each of ground cloves, black pepper, salt to taste, 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil or ghee, 1/2 cup of plain yogurt, 1/4 cup each of cilantro, mint, raisins, and cashews. Start by rinsing and soaking the rice in water for at least 30 minutes. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth or water to a boil and add the soaked rice and a pinch of salt. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the rice for 18-20 minutes or until tender.

In a separate pan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat and add the onions, cooking until golden brown. Add the garlic, ginger, and all the powdered spices and cook for 1 minute. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides. Add the yogurt and raisins and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Spread half of the cooked rice in the bottom of a large casserole dish or pot, and spread the chicken and yogurt mixture over the rice. Layer the remaining rice on top of the chicken, sprinkle the mint, cilantro, cashews over the top. Cover and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious chicken biryani!
